White spot (WSSV)

There is currently no specific treatment for white spot disease in prawns. Prevention and management strategies focus on biosecurity measures to reduce the risk of disease transmission. These include quarantine, strict biosecurity protocols, proper farm management practices, genetic selection for resistance, and regular monitoring and surveillance for early detection. Collaboration with aquatic animal health professionals and regulatory authorities is crucial for developing effective disease management strategies tailored to individual farm conditions.

Black Gill

Treatment options for black gill disease in prawns are limited. Management strategies include maintaining optimal water quality, implementing strict biosecurity measures, using antimicrobial treatments cautiously, considering genetic selection for resistance, and modifying environmental conditions to reduce parasite prevalence. Collaboration with aquatic animal health professionals is crucial for effective disease management.

Vibrosis

Antibiotics are the most common treatment for vibriosis in crustaceans, including prawns, and are used both as prophylaxis and therapeutics. However, improper use of antibiotics can lead to antibiotic resistance and multidrug-resistant (MDR) bacteria. Other methods to reduce the impacts of vibriosis include:Vaccination Water quality control,Bacteriophages or phage therapy, Biosecurity protocols,Herbal immunomodulators,Herbs as antibiotics,Probiotics
